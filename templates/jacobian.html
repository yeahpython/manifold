What the fuck is a Jacobian?

Note: This is best explained in person. If you were able to find a person, they would say something similar to the following: Say you have a function f from one space A to another space B. The Jacobian of f, which we sometimes write as J f, is new function that, for each element x of A, gives us a linear approxmation of the function in a neighborhood of x. In the demo below, we have the composition of two functions. The red and green lines represent linear approximations of the function and may be explicitly recorded in terms of a matrix for each x.
